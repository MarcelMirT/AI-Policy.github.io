Course Overview and Rationale

The rapid development and deployment of Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies have disrupted traditional legal frameworks, making existing legislation and case law obsolete or conflicting with new AI capabilities. This course explores the new legal paradigms and addresses the urgent need to regulate these gaps and establish technical and safety agreements in AI, which pose unique challenges and opportunities for policymakers and regulators. The course focuses on the different legislative approaches adopted by key global players including the United States, the European Union, the United Kingdom, China, and the framework of international agreements. By examining these diverse regulatory environments, participants will gain a comprehensive understanding of how different regions address the ethical, economic, safety, and social implications of AI technologies while trying to promote innovation. By the end of this course, participants will have a strong understanding of the nuanced implications and challenges of AI on key preexisting laws and how different regions are attempting to shape the future of AI and mitigate current legal and social concerns.

Sessions

Session 1: AI Policy Introduction

This session lays the groundwork for the course by introducing key areas of conflict with existing laws, legal principles, and key challenges of AI for regulators that will be explored. This session sets the stage for deeper discussions on how different regions approach AI governance. Participants will explore what AI policy encompasses, including ethical considerations, privacy issues, and the balance between innovation and regulation.

Readings:

  • Racing Through a Minefield: The AI Deployment Problem
  • Overview of Several Issues
  • UN Resolution
  • A Comparative Perspective on AI Regulation
  • Existing Laws Apply AI

Session 2: Data Privacy Regulations

This session focuses on the crucial aspect of data privacy in the context of AI. Participants will explore how AI technologies intersect with data privacy laws, the challenges of ensuring data protection in AI training and applications, and the strategies for compliance with regulations like the GDPR and CCPA.

Readings:

  • GDPR Overview
  • CCPA Overview
  • Data Privacy and AI

Exercises:

  • Evaluate how AI impacts data privacy regulations and the measures needed to ensure compliance.
  • Discuss the balance between data utility and privacy in AI systems.

Session 3: Intellectual Property (IP) Rights

This session addresses the conflicts and synergies between AI technologies and intellectual property rights, particularly copyright. Participants will examine how AI-generated content challenges existing IP frameworks and explore potential solutions for harmonizing AI innovations with IP laws. This session also explores the different approaches and interpretation of copyright protection across key jurisdictions for text and data mining practices used to gather data to train AI models.

Readings:

  • AI and Copyright
  • Text and Data Mining Articles
  • AI and IP Rights
  • A Comparative Perspective on AI Regulation

Exercises:

  • Analyze a specific case where AI-generated content has led to IP conflicts and propose potential legal solutions.
  • Discuss the implications of AI on future IP rights frameworks.

Session 4: AI Use in Decision-Making Tools & Processes

This session will cover the risks and challenges of the adoption of AI in decision-making processes across various fields such as employment law, credit and lending, and housing and real estate. Participants will explore how these products exploit existing regulations (such as anti-discriminatory or the right to an explanation), their social implications, and the regulatory challenges they present. AI systems must comply with anti-discrimination regulations and ensure fairness in their decisions.

Readings:

  • Machine Bias: Risk Assessments in Criminal Sentencing

Exercises:

  • Choose an area where you believe that the adoption of decision-making tools is particularly critical, analyze why, and draw out a legal plan to mitigate such risks. Examples include Employment Law, Credit and Lending, and Housing and Real Estate.

Session 9: China & Their Fragmented Approach to AI Regulations

This session focuses on China's unique approach to AI regulation. Despite the extended belief in an existing obscurity and lack of regulation of AI in China, China has been promoting and adopting a regulatory approach similar to the EU. This includes a particular focus on regulating the commercialization of AI products and protecting Chinese citizens from the negative effects of such technology. We will discuss how China's AI strategies affect international AI dynamics.

Readings:

  • Chip Wars: China Compute
  • China's Draft Regulations on Generative AI
  • China's AI Regulations
  • Translation: Measures for the Management of Generative Artificial Intelligence Services

Session 10: International Agreements

The final session explores the role of international agreements in governing AI and the global efforts that have been placed so far in this direction. As AI technologies do not respect national borders, international cooperation is crucial. We will discuss existing frameworks like the OECD AI Principles, potential global treaties, and the challenges of achieving international consensus on AI standards.

Readings:

  • OECD AI Principles
  • Global AI Treaties
  • Challenges in AI Standardization
