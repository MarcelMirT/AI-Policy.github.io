Course Overview and Rationale

The rapid development and deployment of Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies have disrupted traditional legal frameworks, making existing legislation and case law obsolete or conflicting with new AI capabilities.

This course explores:
(1) This new legal paradigm and the urgent need to regulate these gaps and establish technical and safety agreements in AI.
(2) The different legislator approaches adopted so far by the key global players including the United Sates, European Union, United Kingdom and China and the unique challenges and opportunities for policymakers and regulators.

By examining these diverse regulatory environments, participants will gain a comprehensive understanding of how different regions are addressing the ethical, economic, safety and social implications of AI technologies while trying to promote innovation.

By the end of this course, participants will have a strong understanding of the nuance implications and challenges of AI on key pre-existing laws and how different regions are attempting to shape the future of AI and mitigate current legal and social concerns.

Sessions

Session 1: AI Policy Introduction

This session lays the groundwork for the course by introducing key areas of conflict with existing laws, legal principles, and key challenges of AI for regulators that will be explored. This session sets the stage for deeper discussions on how different regions approach AI governance.

Readings:

  • Racing Through a Minefield: The AI Deployment Problem
  • Overview of Several Issues
  • UN Resolution
  • A Comparative Perspective on AI Regulation
  • Existing Laws Apply AI

Session 2: Data Privacy Regulations

This session focuses on the crucial aspect of data privacy in the context of AI. Participants will explore how AI technologies intersect with data privacy laws, the challenges of ensuring data protection in AI training and applications, and the strategies for compliance with regulations like the GDPR and CCPA.

Readings:

  • GDPR Overview
  • CCPA Overview
  • GDPR Articles:
    • Article 5: Data Processing Principles
    • Article 6: Lawfulness of Processing
    • Article 7: Conditions for Consent
    • Article 11: Processing That Does Not Require Identification
    • Article 14: Information to Be Provided to Data Subject
    • Article 15: Right to Access
    • Article 24: Responsibility of Controller

Exercises:

  • Evaluate how AI impacts data privacy regulations and the measures needed to ensure compliance.
  • Discuss the balance between data utility and privacy in AI systems.

Session 3: Intellectual Property (IP) Rights

This session addresses the conflicts and synergies between AI technologies and intellectual property rights, particularly copyright. Participants will examine how AI-generated content challenges existing IP frameworks and explore potential solutions for harmonizing AI innovations with IP laws. This session also explores the different approaches and interpretation of copyright protection across key jurisdictions for text and data mining practices used to gather data to train AI models.

Readings:

  • AI and Copyright
  • Text and Data Mining Articles
  • AI and IP Rights
  • A Comparative Perspective on AI Regulation
  • AI and IP Rights Overview
  • NY Times: AI and Copyright Lawsuit

Exercises:

  • Analyze a specific case where AI-generated content has led to legal conflicts and propose potential legal solutions. Examples:
    • Content Generation and Repurposing
    • Deepfakes and Synthetic Media
    • AI in Music Composition
    • AI-driven Art and Graphic Design
    • Machine Learning Models Trained on Copyrighted Data
    • Text and Data Mining Tools
  • Discuss the implications of AI on future IP rights frameworks.

Session 4: AI Use in Decision-Making Tools & Processes

This session will cover the risks and challenges of the adoption of AI in decision-making processes across various fields such as employment law, the judicial system, credit and lending, and housing and real estate. Participants will explore how these products can exploit existing regulations (such as anti-discriminatory laws or the right to an explanation), their social implications, the opacity of their decision-making processes, and regulatory challenges. Participants will be exposed to recent examples of AI decision-making tools that were proved to be biased or highly inaccurate.

Readings:

  • Machine Bias: Risk Assessments in Criminal Sentencing
  • Springer Article on AI Decision-Making
  • TEDx Talk on AI Bias
  • SAGE Journal Article

Exercises:

  • Choose an area where you believe that the adoption of decision-making tools is particularly critical, analyze why, and draw out a legal plan to mitigate such risks. Examples include:
    • Employment Law: AI in hiring processes must comply with anti-discrimination regulations.
    • Credit and Lending: AI determining creditworthiness must avoid discriminatory practices.
    • Housing and Real Estate: AI tools in property valuation and mortgage lending must ensure fair treatment.

Session 5: Liability Allocation

In this session, we will explore the complex and critical issue of liability allocation in the context of Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems. As AI technologies become increasingly integrated into various sectors, determining who is responsible when these systems cause harm or fail to perform as expected becomes paramount. This includes establishing the liability regime for downstream developers and “fine-tuners” of frontier models and a duty of care for the main developer to oversee the downstream uses of its system. Clear liability allocation is an essential legal requirement for the effective and successful implementation of all legal frameworks across every domain. This session will examine the different legal frameworks and approaches to allocating liability for AI-induced damages, considering perspectives from various jurisdictions. Participants will delve into the key challenges of attributing liability and different liability models and their applicability in the context of AI.

Readings:

  • Journal of AI Research: Liability in AI
  • European Parliament Brief on AI Liability
  • ScienceDirect: AI and Liability

Exercises:

  • Analyze a case study where AI malfunction led to significant damages. Identify the stakeholders involved and propose a liability allocation framework.
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of current liability laws in your jurisdiction for addressing AI-related harm. Suggest improvements or alternative models that could be more effective.

Session 6: The US Landscape

In this session, we will delve into the regulatory framework and current plans for governing AI in the United States. This session will also touch on key legislation, federal guidelines, and key US concerns.

Readings:

  • CISA: Recent Efforts in AI
  • Preventing AI Chip Smuggling to China
  • Executive Order on AI
  • Politico Article on AI Regulation

Session 7: The EU Framework & The EU AI Act

This session will focus on the European Union’s AI Act, one of the pioneer regulations worldwide, and how it addresses the initial EU concerns with AI products. Participants will learn about the Act's scope, history, evolution, and rationale, its categorization of AI risks, requirements for high-risk AI systems, and implications for the commercialization of AI products. The session will also discuss the global impact of the EU AI Act as a potential standard-setter in AI regulation.

Readings:

  • EU AI Act Website
  • AI Act Explorer
  • High-Level Summary
  • Global Regulatory Tracker
  • ArXiv: EU AI Act Analysis

Key Articles:

  • Article 13: Transparency and Provision of Information to Deployers
  • Article 14: Human Oversight
  • Article 15: Accuracy, Robustness, and Cybersecurity
  • Article 10: Data and Data Governance
  • Article 50: Transparency Obligations for Providers and Users of Certain AI Systems and GPAI Models

Exercises:

  • Analyze a principle or article of the EU AI Act, its intentions, justification, probable impact, challenges for implementation, and long-term implications or conflicts with the current or future state of AI technology.
  • Choose a specific right from a non-AI text (e.g., privacy, copyright) and analyze how AI impacts this regulation, enables new ways of exploiting it, challenges its application, or forces a modification or different interpretation in the law.

Session 8: The UK

Post-Brexit, the UK has been carving its path in AI regulation. This session will examine the UK's key role and huge importance within AI and its approach to AI governance. We will discuss specific regulatory initiatives such as the UK’s National AI Strategy and how these policies support the UK's vision to be a leader in AI and AI safety research.

Readings:

  • UK Government AI Regulation Policy
  • AI Risk Briefing Paper
  • Deloitte: The UK’s Framework for AI Regulation

Session 9: China & Their Fragmented Approach to AI Regulations

This session focuses on China's unique approach to AI regulation. Despite the extended belief in an existing obscurity and lack of regulation of AI in China, China has been promoting and adopting a regulatory approach similar to the EU. This includes a particular focus on regulating the commercialization of AI products and protecting Chinese citizens from the negative effects of such technology. We will discuss how China's AI strategies affect international AI dynamics.

Readings:

  • Chip Wars: China Compute
  • China's Draft Regulations on Generative AI
  • China's AI Regulations
  • Translation: Measures for the Management of Generative Artificial Intelligence Services
  • China Law Translate: Algorithms

Session 10: International Agreements

The final session explores the role of international agreements in governing AI and the global efforts that have been placed so far in this direction. As AI technologies do not respect national borders, international cooperation is crucial. We will discuss existing frameworks like the OECD AI Principles, potential global treaties, and the challenges of achieving international consensus on AI standards.

Readings:

  • OECD AI Principles
  • Global AI Treaties
  • Council of Europe: AI Standards
  • The Bletchley Declaration
  • Freshfields: International Treaty on AI
